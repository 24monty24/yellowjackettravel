Atlanta's History

pre-1820

1762

The Native American site, Standing Peachtree, situated at the border of the Creek and Cherokee territories was first mentioned in recorded history.

1820-1859

1821

The Creek Indians cede their land to the United States. This land is now the metro Atlanta area.

1836

The terminus of the planned Western and Atlantic Railroad is marked. This spot was located near today's Five Points neighbrhood.

1842

The Terminus settlement is incorporated as Marthasville. Named after the daughter of Governor Wilson Lumpkin.

1847

Atlanta is incorporated.

1854

Atlanta and La Grange Railroad connects Atlanta with the southwest. This helps make Atlanta the rail hub for the South with connections to the northwest, east, southeast and southwest.

1860-1889

1861-1865

The American Civil War. As a major railroad and supply hub in the South, Atlanta plays a pivotal role in the war.

1863

President Abraham Lincoln signs the Emancipation Proclamation, freeing over 3 million slaves nationally.

January 1

1864

Battle of Atlanta, part of the Union's Atlanta Campaign is fought. Although a major win for the Union army, the Confederacy still maintained control of the city until September 1.

July 22

During the Battle of Jonesboro Union General William Tecumseh Sherman cuts off the last supply line to Atlanta, forcing Confederate General John Bell Hood to abandon the city.

August 31-September 1

General Sherman's troops burn Atlanta to the ground, sparing only churches and hospitals.

November 11

1865

The American Civil War ends. President Andrew Johnson officially declares the end of the war.

May 9

Atlanta University is founded. This was Atlanta's first college to serve African-American studends.

1868

Atlanta becomes the state capital of Georgia.

1880

Atlanta surpasses Savannah to become Georgia's largest city.

1885

Governor Henry D. McDaniel signs the bill founding the Georgia School of Technology, now named the Georgia Institute of Technology.

October 13

1886

The first Coca-Cola is sold at Jacob's Pharmacy. The development of Coca-Cola was in response to Atlanta and Fulton County becoming "dry". Coca-Cola was a substitute for a previous coca wine (alcohol and cocaine) created by John Pemberton.

May 8

1887

Inman Park is the first planned suburb of Atlanta.

1889

The Georgia State Capitol building opens. The dome was originally constructed of terra cotta and tin. A 1958 renovation gave the building the gold leaf gilding that it's recognized for today.

xxxxx

1890-1939

1895

Booker T. Washington delivers his "Atlanta Compromise" speech during the Cotton States and International Exposition

September 18

1906

Atlanta Race Riot of 1906. Tensions between black and white workers were high as many flocked to Atlanta in search of employment. On September 22 the Atlanta Georgia and Atlanta News reported on the alledged rape of four white women by black men. By the end of the riot it is believed that 27 people died.

September 22-24

1917

The Great Atlanta Fire of 1917. Beginning the morning of May 21 four separate fires broke out throughout Atlanta. When crews arrived at the fourth fire they found a stack of burning mattresses but were not equipped to put it out. The fire quickly spread. By the time the fire was put out it had travelled over a mile to the north of where it began, destroying everything in its path.

May 21

1936

Techwood Homes, the nation's first public housing development was completed.

August 15

1939

Gone With the Wind, a film adaptation of Margaret Mitchell's novel premiered at Loew's Grand Theatre. Mitchell was an Atlanta native and wrote her novel in her home located on Peachtree Street.

December 15

1940-present

1959

Population of the Atlanta metropolitan area reaches 1 million.

1973

Maynard Holbrook Jackson Jr. is elected mayork of Atlanta, becoming the city's first black mayor, and any other major city in the South.

1992

Bank of America Plaza, formerly NationsBank Plaza, is completed. At the time of its completion it was the tallest U.S. building outside of New York and Chicago. It is still the tallest building in the South.

1996

Atlanta was host to the 1996 Summer Olympics, officially the Games of the XXVI Olympiad and unofficially the Centennial Olympic Games. A record 197 nations participated, all of the current members of the International Olympic Committee.

July 19-August 4

2005

Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport becomes the world's busiest airport in passenger traffic.

2011

In 1936 the nation's first public housing development was built in Atlanta, but in 2011 Atlanta became the first major U.S. city to completely remove all public housing developments.
